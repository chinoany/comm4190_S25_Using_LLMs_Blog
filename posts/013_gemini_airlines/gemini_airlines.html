Gemini Airlines: Having Google’s Gemini Plan My Next Vacation

Introduction

I’m someone who loves to travel every chance I get, but planning a trip can feel like a second job! While staring at flight options for my next getaway, it realized I dont have to do all of this, AI exists! Why not hand the logistics over to AI? So I asked Google’s Gemini to design a one week solo vacation for me from scratch: flights, hotel/airbnb, things to do, etc. Here’s how Gemini did at playing full-service travel agent!

Experiment Setup

  • Model: Google’s Gemini 2.0 Flash

The Prompt I Gave Gemini

Prompt:
Plan a one-week trip for a 21 year old black female college student. My budget is 2000 including flights. I’m a US citizen.

Gemini’s Response 1: It sounds like you’re looking for an exciting and budget-friendly one-week trip! As a 21-year-old Black female college student, you’ll want a destination that’s safe, offers interesting experiences, and fits within your $2000 budget, including flights. Here are a few ideas focusing on destinations within the United States that can offer a fantastic week without breaking the bank:

Option 1: New Orleans, Louisiana - Culture, Music, and Delicious Food

Why it’s great: New Orleans is a vibrant city with a unique blend of cultures, incredible live music (especially jazz!), delicious and affordable food, and a rich history. It’s also known for its friendly atmosphere. Things to do: Wander through the historic French Quarter, admiring the architecture and soaking in the lively atmosphere. Street performers are always entertaining! Listen to live jazz on Frenchmen Street – there are numerous clubs with affordable cover charges or free entry. Take a stroll through the beautiful Garden District and admire the stunning mansions. You can even do a self-guided walking tour. Indulge in the city’s famous cuisine: beignets at Cafe Du Monde, po’boys, gumbo, and jambalaya are must-tries and can be found at various price points. Consider a swamp tour to see alligators and learn about the unique Louisiana wetlands. Visit the New Orleans Museum of Art or the Backstreet Cultural Museum to learn about the city’s rich cultural heritage, including its African American history. Budget Considerations: Flights: Depending on your departure city, round-trip flights within the US can often be found for under $400-$500 if booked in advance and during off-peak times. Accommodation: Hostels are a budget-friendly option, with private rooms sometimes available. Look for options in areas like the Marigny or near the French Quarter. Budget hotels or Airbnb stays slightly outside the main tourist areas can also be affordable. Aim for around $50-$80 per night. Food: New Orleans offers many affordable food options, from casual eateries to food trucks. You can easily budget $30-$50 per day for delicious meals. Activities: Many attractions, like walking around the French Quarter and listening to street music, are free. Museums and tours have entrance fees, so prioritize what interests you most. Look for free events or discounted days. Transportation: The city is walkable in many areas. Public transportation (streetcars and buses) is also available and affordable. Option 2: Chicago, Illinois - Architecture, Museums, and City Life

Why it’s great: Chicago boasts stunning architecture, world-class museums, diverse neighborhoods, and a vibrant arts and culture scene. It’s a big city with plenty to explore without needing a car for everything. Things to do… (Very long output)

Observation: It provided a lot of information… almost too much. I also wanted to travel internationally so I prompted it again.

Second Prompt

Prompt 2: i want to travel internationally

Gemini’s Response 2: (Gave me paragraphs of information on Option 1: Mexico and Option 2: Colombia and Option 3: Portugal) lets focus on option 3 … Option 3: Portugal - History, Beaches, and Delicious Food (Lisbon or Porto)

Why it’s great: Portugal offers a blend of historical cities, beautiful beaches (especially in the Algarve region, though that might be better for a longer trip), delicious seafood, and a relatively affordable cost of living compared to other Western European countries. Possible Destinations within Portugal: Lisbon: A charming capital city with colorful buildings, historic neighborhoods (like Alfama and Bairro Alto), stunning viewpoints, and delicious pastries (pastel de nata!). Porto: Known for its port wine cellars, beautiful bridges, and historic Ribeira district along the Douro River. Budget Considerations: Flights: Flights to Portugal from the US can vary greatly. You might find round-trip options in the $500-$800 range, especially if you fly into larger hubs like Lisbon or Porto and are flexible. Accommodation: Hostels are a good budget option in both Lisbon and Porto (around $20-$40 per night). Budget guesthouses (pensões) and Airbnb rooms can also be found for reasonable prices ($40-$70 per night). Food: Eating at local tascas (small restaurants) can be very affordable and offers delicious traditional Portuguese food. Budget $30-$50 per day for food. Activities: Walking around the cities, exploring historic neighborhoods, and enjoying viewpoints are free. Public transportation is efficient and relatively inexpensive. Entrance fees to attractions are generally reasonable.

Observation: I loved Option 3 because I’ve never been to Portugal so Lisbon would be a new experience for me.

What Gemini Nailed vs. Things I Would Change

  • Affordable, photogenic city with English signage
  • Afro-diaspora tour + Mozambican food = identity-aware choices (Gemini considered things I might like as an African-American)
  • Safety tips on pickpockets, late-night rideshares. However, no mention of occasional anti-Black bias reports, would require personal research worth personal research.
  • Zero suggestions for hostel meet-ups or social apps if I want company.

Key Takeaways!

  1. Context matters. Age, identity, and budget steered Gemini toward a culturally relevant, safe, and affordable city.
  2. Almost book-ready. Only minor edits (visa reminder, bigger food cushion) separate this plan from purchase clicks.
  3. Safety nuance: decent, not deep. Petty-crime pointers are there; bias insights require human legwork.

Why Does This Matter?

Even the most seasoned travelers still lose hours piecing together flights, lodgings, and daily routes. Gemini can cut that time to minutes, freeing people to focus on planning outfits and counting the days down till vacation!
